1.Subhas Chandra Bose, popularly known as "Netaji," was a prominent Indian nationalist and freedom fighter.
2.He was born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha, into a well-educated and influential family.
3.Bose was known for his fierce patriotism and revolutionary approach to achieving India's independence from British rule.
4.A brilliant student, he passed the Indian Civil Services (ICS) exam but resigned in 1921 to join the freedom struggle.
5.He became the President of the Indian National Congress twice but resigned due to ideological differences with Mahatma Gandhi and others.
6.Netaji formed the Forward Bloc in 1939 and later led the Indian National Army (INA) to fight against the British during World War II.
7.His famous slogan, "Give me blood, and I will give you freedom," inspired millions of Indians.
8.Bose sought international support from Axis powers like Germany and Japan to liberate India.
9.He is believed to have died in a plane crash on August 18, 1945, although the circumstances remain controversial.
10.Subhas Chandra Bose remains a revered figure in India for his courage, vision, and unwavering commitment to freedom.
